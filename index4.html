Decription Lists

  • Artist: Performed by Zac Efron and Zendaya
  • Film: Featured in the 2017 American musical biographical drama film, The Greatest Showman.
  • Original Soundtrack: Included on the films official soundtrack album, The Greatest Showman: Original Motion Picture Soundtrack.
  • Release Date: The soundtrack was released on December 8, 2017.
  • Songwriters: Written by Benj Pasek and Justin Paul.
  • Producers: Produced by Greg Wells, Benj Pasek, and Justin Paul
  • Genre: Pop, Musical Theatre, Power Ballad.
  • Vocals: Sung as a duet between Zac Efron (as Phillip Carlyle) and Zendaya (as Anne Wheeler).
  • Lyrical Content: The song is a passionate and emotionally charged exchange between the two characters, Phillip and Anne. It centers on the theme of star-crossed lovers and the societal obstacles they face in their relationship. Phillip, a privileged and wealthy man, attempts to convince Anne, a trapeze artist and woman of color, that they can overcome the social barriers and "rewrite the stars" to be together. Anne, however, expresses her fear and the reality that their love is forbidden by the social norms of the time.
  • Musical Style: The song features a driving, rhythmic acoustic guitar and a soaring, powerful chorus. It builds in intensity as the two characters' emotions escalate.
  • Performance in Film: The song is performed during a visually stunning and dynamic scene in the film. The choreography involves Anne Wheeler's aerialist skills, with her and Phillip Carlyle dancing and singing while suspended in the air.
  • Commercial Performance: The song was a major commercial success, reaching high chart positions in numerous countries. It was certified multi-platinum in the United States and the United Kingdom, among other places.
  • Awards and Recognition: The song's composers, Pasek and Paul, received a Golden Globe nomination for Best Original Song. The song was also a significant part of the soundtrack's success, which won a Grammy Award for Best Compilation Soundtrack for Visual Media.
  • Remix: A version of the song was recorded by James Arthur and Anne-Marie for the album The Greatest Showman: Reimagined, which featured new renditions of the film's songs by various artists.
